# Using AI Agents for Real Work

I run six content websites alongside looking for my next role. Without automation, that would be impossible. Here's how I actually use AI agents day to day.

## Job scanning

I built an automated job scan that runs daily. It searches LinkedIn and recruiter sites, scores each role against my criteria (sector fit, salary, location, culture), and produces a ranked shortlist. What used to take 45 minutes of scrolling now takes two minutes of reading.

## Content pipelines

Each of my sites publishes evidence-based articles. The pipeline works like this:

1. AI researches the topic and pulls relevant clinical trials
2. It drafts an article following my editorial guidelines
3. I review, edit, and approve before publishing
4. SEO metadata and structured data are generated automatically

The key is the review step. AI does the heavy lifting on research and first drafts. I do the editorial judgement and fact-checking. Nothing publishes without my sign-off.

## Site management

I use MCP server integrations to connect Claude Code with Gmail, MailerLite, Vercel, and Notion. This means I can manage deployments, check email subscriber stats, and update site content from one interface.

## What I've learned

AI agents are genuinely useful when you treat them as tools, not magic. They're good at repetitive tasks, research aggregation, and first drafts. They're bad at editorial judgement, nuance, and knowing when something sounds wrong.

The 90+ articles across my sites were all researched and drafted with AI help, then reviewed and edited by me. That's the model that works: AI handles the legwork, I make the decisions.
