# Neurodivergence at Work: Building Systems That Work for Your Brain

Being neurodivergent in tech isn't a disadvantage—it's a different operating system. Once I stopped trying to work like neurotypical colleagues and started building systems that match how my brain actually works, everything changed.

## The ADHD Advantage in Tech Ops

Hyperfocus is real. When I'm in the zone, I can troubleshoot complex infrastructure issues that would take others days. The trick is creating conditions where hyperfocus can happen reliably.

### My System

**External memory is everything.** I don't trust my brain to hold tasks, so I built systems that do it for me:

- **Obsidian for knowledge** - Every solution documented immediately
- **Automated reminders** - If it's not in the calendar, it doesn't exist
- **Body doubling** - Virtual coworking sessions for accountability
- **Time blocking** - Deep work in the morning, meetings after lunch

## Accommodations That Actually Help

Forget generic "flexible working" policies. Here's what made a real difference:

1. **Async-first communication** - Slack threads, not impromptu calls
2. **Written requirements** - Verbal briefs get lost; docs don't
3. **Focus time protection** - No meetings before 2 PM
4. **Clear escalation paths** - Ambiguity is the enemy

## The Passive Income Connection

Building passive income sites turned out to be perfect for my brain. I can work in intense bursts, automate the repetitive stuff, and let systems run while I recover. No corporate meeting schedule to fight against.

## Advice for Other Neurodivergent Folks

1. **Stop masking** - Energy spent hiding your traits is energy not spent on actual work
2. **Build your scaffolding** - Systems beat willpower every time
3. **Find your niche** - Some environments amplify neurodivergent strengths
4. **Own your superpowers** - Pattern recognition, creative problem-solving, resilience
