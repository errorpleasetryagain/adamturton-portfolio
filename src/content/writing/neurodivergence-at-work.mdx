# Working With ADHD in Tech Operations

I was diagnosed with ADHD and autism (level 1) in my thirties. By then I'd already spent years in tech ops roles, building workarounds without knowing what I was working around.

## What actually helps

Three things make the biggest difference for me:

1. **Written communication over meetings.** I process information better when I can read it, re-read it, and respond thoughtfully. Slack and documentation beat hour-long calls.

2. **Autonomy over micromanagement.** I work in bursts. Some days I'll produce two days' worth of output in four hours. Other days I need to step away and come back. The output is consistent; the schedule isn't always.

3. **Clear priorities.** When everything is urgent, nothing gets done. I need to know what matters most, then I'll handle it.

## What doesn't help

Being told to "just focus." Having my working patterns compared to neurotypical norms. Open-plan offices with no quiet spaces. Meetings that could have been emails.

## Why I mention it

Not to make a thing of it. It's just how my brain works. I mention it because I'd rather work somewhere that knows upfront and is fine with it, than somewhere that finds out later and isn't.

The best teams I've worked on had a mix of working styles. The worst ones expected everyone to operate identically.
