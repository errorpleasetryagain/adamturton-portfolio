# Building Resilient Tech Operations

After managing technology operations at Starling Bank, supporting 800+ employees across critical banking infrastructure, a few things became clear about what actually makes operations work.

## The basics matter more than the tools

Every vendor sells a silver bullet. In practice, the teams that recover fastest from incidents are the ones with clear runbooks, sensible escalation paths, and people who trust each other enough to say "I don't know" without embarrassment.

We reduced mean time to resolution by 40% at Starling. The biggest single change wasn't a new tool. It was rewriting our escalation matrix so the right person got paged first, not third.

## Automate the boring parts

If something breaks twice and the fix is the same both times, script it. We also went through our vendor contracts and cut tooling we didn't need. Most of that wasn't clever negotiation. It was noticing we were paying for three tools that did the same thing.

## The human side

Tech ops is a people job disguised as a systems job. The best thing I did as a manager was run weekly one-to-ones that were genuinely about development, not status updates. When your team trusts you, they flag problems earlier. Earlier flags mean smaller incidents.

## What I'd do differently

I'd invest in observability earlier. We were reactive for too long before building proper dashboards. And I'd push harder for blameless post-mortems from day one, rather than introducing them six months in.
