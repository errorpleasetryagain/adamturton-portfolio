# Most team problems start with trust

This is not my writing. It is a pointer to Patrick Lencioni's The Five Dysfunctions of a Team, with a few words on why I keep coming back to it.

Lencioni stacks team problems into a pyramid, and trust sits at the bottom. Without it you get no real conflict, just polite silence. Without honest conflict you get no commitment, because people never actually said what they thought. Without commitment there is no accountability, and without accountability nobody focuses on results. Pull the trust block out and the whole thing falls over.

What I like is how practical it is. It is easy to blame a struggling team on "results" when the real problem is two levels down, that people do not trust each other enough to disagree openly. I have watched teams transform not by working harder but by getting honest with each other in a way they had been avoiding.

For a tech ops team this is everything. When something breaks at 2am, you need people who will say "I think I caused this" without fear, and who will challenge a bad call before it ships. That only happens if you have built the trust first.

If you lead a team, it is worth your time. Read the original at the link above.
